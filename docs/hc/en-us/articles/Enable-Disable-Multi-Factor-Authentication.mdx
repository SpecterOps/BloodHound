## Purpose


This article describes how to enable/disable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for a BloodHound user configured for built-in authentication.

## Process


1.  Log into your BloodHound tenant.
2.  In the top right, click settings <Icon icon="gear"iconType="solid"/> <Icon icon="arrow-right"iconType="solid"/>Â **My Profile**
<Frame>
  <img src="/hc/images/image-2-2.png"/>
</Frame>

3. Toggle the Multi-Factor Authentication switch

  <Frame>
    <img src="/hc/images/image-2-3.png"/>
</Frame>

4.  Continue in one of the two headings below:
    * [Enabling MFA](#h_01HTJDE0SANNG8DTEBVWK03RB2)
    * [Disabling MFA](#h_01HTJDE5C3X0QXRDZWPA8XM0G9)

### **Enabling MFA**

1.  In the pop-up, confirm your user's password and click on **Next**
<Frame>
<img src="/hc/images/image-2-4.png"/>
</Frame>
2.  Scan the QR code with your multi-factor authentication application, enter the 6-digit one-time password, and click **Next**
<Frame>
<img src="/hc/images/image-2-5.png"/>
</Frame>
3.  Multi-factor authentication is now enabled, click **Close**
<Frame>
<img src="/hc/images/image-2-6.png"/>
</Frame>

### **Disabling MFA**

1.  In the pop-up, confirm your user's password and click on **Disable Multi-Factor Authentication**
<Frame>
<img src="/hc/images/image-2-7.png"/>
</Frame>
2.  Multi-factor authentication is now disabled

## Outcome

If enabling MFA, next time you log in, you'll need to use both your password and authentication code.

If you lose your authentication code device, you'll need to contact an Administrator of your BloodHound tenant who can reset your MFA configuration.
